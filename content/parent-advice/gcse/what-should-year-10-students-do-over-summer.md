Year 10 students should use the summer to stabilise weak GCSE topics, rebuild routines, and practise calmly. The aim is not to recreate school at home, but to make Year 11 feel less rushed.

## What matters most

Parents should start by finding the subjects where marks are being lost repeatedly. In Maths and Science, this often means a small number of weak topics, poor recall, or unclear exam method.

## A sensible summer pattern

- Review recent tests or mock papers.
- Choose two or three priority topics per subject.
- Practise little and often rather than cramming.
- Keep one clear record of mistakes and corrections.
- Leave proper rest time so September does not begin with burnout.

## When to ask for help

If your child is working but the marks are not moving, the issue may be diagnosis rather than effort. A short consultation can help identify whether they need content repair, exam practice, or a clearer study routine.
