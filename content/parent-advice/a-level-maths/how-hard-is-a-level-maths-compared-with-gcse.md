A-Level Maths is a significant step up from GCSE Maths. The biggest change is not just harder content, but faster pace, greater independence, and questions that combine several ideas at once.

## Why the jump feels difficult

At GCSE, many students can succeed by learning familiar methods and practising common question types. At A-Level, they need stronger algebra, better notation, and more confidence with unfamiliar problems.

## Signs a student may need support

- They achieved well at GCSE but feel slow in Year 12.
- Algebra errors are appearing in many topics.
- They understand worked examples but struggle alone.
- Homework takes much longer than expected.

## What makes the transition easier

Students usually need a steady routine, early correction of weak algebra, and regular practice that moves beyond memorising steps. It is better to address the jump early than wait until confidence has already dropped.
