A child can lack confidence in Maths even when they are capable. Often the problem is not ability, but repeated small gaps, rushed explanations, or a few public mistakes that make the subject feel unsafe.

## Common reasons confidence drops

Maths builds in layers. If fractions, algebra, negative numbers, or problem-solving steps are shaky, later topics can feel much harder than they should.

## What parents can look for

- Avoiding homework until the last minute.
- Saying "I get it in class" but losing marks in tests.
- Making careless errors when working under time pressure.
- Giving up quickly when a question looks unfamiliar.

## What helps

Confidence usually improves when students experience clear teaching, manageable practice, and visible correction. The goal is to help them see exactly what went wrong and what to do differently next time.
