## The one-sentence verdict

**The site is professionally built and well-organised, but it sounds like a consultancy, not like Jothi.** Every sentence on the homepage could appear on any tutoring website in the UK. The things that make Jothi genuinely unfakeable — Classkick live supervision, the 4T system, your radical-honesty promise, your 5-year track record, your actual results, your face, your story, and the Indian diaspora parent you serve best — are either missing, hidden, or watered down into corporate language.

**The good news:** fixing this is mostly a copy, structure, and proof-point job. The technical foundation is solid. You can move from "generic tutoring site" to "the obvious choice for my anxious mid-market parent" with no redesign — just sharper content, better proof, and two CTAs the site currently doesn't offer.

---

## What the site does well (preserve this)

Before pulling things apart, five things are already working and must be protected in any rebuild:

1. **Clean, fast, static HTML foundation.** No bloated frameworks. Good for speed, SEO, and easy editing via Claude Code.
2. **Proper semantic structure.** Skip-to-content link present, logical nav, footer with legal/company details, ICO registration displayed — this is serious trust hygiene that many competitors skip.
3. **Site structure is sensible.** Programmes / About / Team / Results / Contact is exactly the right spine.
4. **The pricing model described in the long-form brochure text** (40 lessons, 8 tests, annual mock, parent consultations, etc.) is genuinely differentiated. Most competitors can't match this structure.
5. **The phrase "more than patchy tutoring"** is the one line on the site that has real bite. Keep it.

That's the foundation. Everything below is about what's missing on top of it.

## Part 1 — The strategic & copy problems (ranked by impact on your ICP)

### Problem 1 — The homepage hero fails the 5-second test

**What it says now:**
> *"Premium academic guidance. Structured Maths and Science programmes that change academic trajectories. When a capable child is working hard but the results still do not reflect it, the problem is rarely effort alone. Families often need a clearer system."*

**Why this is a problem for your ICP:**
- "Premium academic guidance" is a category label, not a promise. It doesn't tell a parent what they'll *get*.
- "Change academic trajectories" is consultancy-speak. A stressed mum at 10pm doesn't think in "trajectories."
- "Families often need a clearer system" is so generic it could be about therapy, financial advice, or dog training.
- **None of your unique proof is in the first screen.** No "5 years, 500+ students, 150 families," no UKMT medals, no A-Level acceleration, no Classkick, no face.

**What "wow" looks like for your specific ICP** (rough draft for direction, not final):

> **Your child's next school report lands soon. You don't want another surprise.**
>
> At Jothi Learning, we turn maths-fearing students into confident ones — and confident students into UKMT medal winners. Not through more tutoring hours, but through a system: live supervision of every lesson, weekly testing, and honest feedback every single week. 150 families trust us with their child's future. Here's how we earn it.
>
> **[WhatsApp Jothi now]**  **[Book a free 45-min diagnostic]**

Notice what this does:
- Starts with the parent's *emotion* (anxiety about the next report)
- Names the two transformation stories you have (fear→confidence, and confident→medallist)
- Makes a specific operational promise (live supervision, weekly tests, honest weekly feedback)
- Uses real numbers (150 families)
- Primary CTA = WhatsApp (your actual primary)
- Secondary CTA = Free diagnostic (already what you do)

### Problem 2 — Your unfakeable moat (Classkick) is invisible

Your own business doc says: *"AI tools and low-cost tutors cannot replicate this live, interactive, evidence-rich environment."*

This is the single strongest competitive statement in your entire business. It appears nowhere on the site.

**What you need:** A whole section on the homepage titled something like *"Every lesson, we watch your child think — live"* with a short explanation, a screenshot of a Classkick session in progress, and a line explaining why this is impossible with AI tutors or marketplace platforms.

This turns a feature into a *defensive moat* in the parent's mind. When they see GoStudent ads later, they'll remember "but GoStudent can't *watch my child work in real time.*"

### Problem 3 — The Results page has no results

The Results page is called "Results and Reviews" but contains:
- Zero numbers
- Zero named students
- Zero measurable outcomes
- Zero UKMT medals listed
- Zero grade data
- Four unsigned "parent reviews" that look like placeholders

For your ICP — structure-loving, data-reassured, anxiety-reducing — this is the most important page after the homepage, and it's the weakest.

**What you need (minimum viable proof for relaunch):**

1. **Headline numbers.** Even rough:
   - *"5 years in operation. 500+ students taught. 150 families active today."*
   - *"X% of GCSE students achieve Grade 7+"* (get your real number)
   - *"X UKMT medals awarded to Jothi students since 2020"*
   - *"1 cohort completing 2-year A-Level course in 10 months (pioneering, ongoing)"*
   - *"X% of students stay with us for 2+ years"* (retention is huge for trust)

2. **Named case studies.** 3–5 minimum, ideally:
   - *"Aarav — Year 9 maths-phobic → Grade 8 at GCSE"*
   - *"Riya — UKMT Silver medal in Year 8"*
   - *"Ethan — Year 12 starting A-Level from scratch, on track to finish in 10 months"*
   - Short narrative (3–4 sentences), a photo (or illustrated avatar if photo permission pending), a named parent quote.

3. **Named, quoted testimonials.** "Parent review" with no name looks fake and actually *reduces* trust. Even first-name-only ("Sarah M., mother of Year 10 student") is vastly better. Get permission from your existing reviewers — email them, call them. Most will say yes. Link the Google reviews page too.

4. **Video testimonials embedded.** You mentioned you have YouTube videos. Embed them. Video testimonials convert 2–3× better than written ones. Get explicit permission now — most parents will agree when asked politely.

## Part 2 — Design & UX observations

### What needs improvement

1. **Hero image is generic.** Stock photo of a student with a notebook. Needs replacing with (a) a photograph of you or a real tutor, or (b) a Classkick screenshot showing live teaching in progress, or (c) both side by side.

2. **No visual diagrams for Trifecta/4T.** Already flagged in Problem 6. These need to be actual graphics, not text labels.

3. **No use of colour to direct attention.** Every CTA button looks the same. Primary CTA (WhatsApp) should be the only bright green button on the page — eye naturally goes to it.

4. **Sections blur together.** Hard to distinguish between "Structured support", "Intro", "Visible structure", "What families value", "Why families stay" — they all say similar things. The site needs *fewer* sections with stronger, more distinct content in each.

5. **No photos of tutors or students.** Warm + nurturing requires faces. Until real photos are available, use consistent AI-generated photography in a clear, documented style (not a mix of clashing aesthetics).

6. **Typography feels corporate rather than warm.** I can't confirm without seeing the live rendering, but the copy suggests a formal serif-led treatment. For "warm premium" you want a softer serif (e.g. Fraunces, Lora) for headlines paired with a humanist sans-serif (e.g. Inter, Source Sans) for body — not a stern traditional serif.

7. **No footer sign-up for PDF guide / newsletter.** With your tertiary CTA ("Download UK education guide"), there's a natural place to capture leads who aren't ready to book.

### Specific page-by-page recommendations

- **Homepage:** Currently ~10 sections. Cut to 6, make each one earn its place. (See Part 4.)
- **Programmes:** Good structure, but every programme block uses identical language — "Best for: students who..." repeats. Needs differentiation through specific outcomes and specific case studies.
- **Results:** Currently the weakest page. Needs a full rebuild around actual numbers and named case studies. (See Problem 3.)
- **Team:** Haven't audited yet — but critical to warm-premium positioning. Should have a photo and 100-word bio for each named tutor, plus DBS status indicator.
- **About:** Should carry the founder story (see Problem 5).
- **Contact:** Should be WhatsApp-first, form second, email third, phone fourth. Currently phone is primary (tel: link), form is secondary, WhatsApp is absent.

## Part 4 — The wow-factor rebuild plan

### The new homepage structure (6 sections, not 10)

1. **Hero** — Emotional promise + two CTAs (WhatsApp primary, Diagnostic secondary)
2. **Social proof band** — "5 years. 500+ students. 150 families today." + 3 headline outcome stats + Google reviews badge
3. **Why Jothi is different** — The Classkick live supervision moat + 4T system + Radical Honesty policy (three visual blocks)
4. **Transformation stories** — Two named case studies (fear→confidence, ok→medallist) with photo + quote
5. **Programmes snapshot** — 4 programme cards with "Best for X / Year Y / £ZZZ" on each
6. **Founder block** — Photo of Prakash + 150-word origin story + diagnostic CTA
7. **FAQ for new-to-UK parents** — 6 questions specifically for Indian diaspora parents (GCSE options, exam boards, Oxbridge, set changes, A-Level combos, university prep)
8. **Final CTA band** — "Next report lands soon. Let's not leave it to chance." + WhatsApp + Diagnostic

(Okay that's 8, not 6 — but each one is pulling weight.)

### The new tone of voice

Six rules for any new copy (past this audit):

1. **Talk to the parent, not about the service.** Start sentences with "you" and "your child" more often than "Jothi" or "we."
2. **Specific > abstract.** "150 families" beats "many families." "UKMT Silver medals" beats "strong outcomes."
3. **Name the emotion you understand.** "You're worried because..." beats any hedged corporate alternative.
4. **Promise the operational truth.** "We watch every lesson live on Classkick" beats "high-quality supervision."
5. **Use short sentences. Then occasionally a long one — to vary the rhythm, carry the reader, and make the next short sentence land. Like this.**
6. **Never use the word 'structured' more than once per page.** The current site uses it 30+ times. It's become wallpaper.
